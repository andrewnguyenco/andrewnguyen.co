About Me

I'm an MD/PhD student passionate about leveraging artificial intelligence and medical imaging to advance early disease detection and personalized medicine. My research focuses on developing novel radiomic biomarkers for lung cancer prediction using perivascular adipose tissue analysis.

Currently funded through an NIH subaward, I work with large-scale medical imaging datasets to create automated segmentation pipelines and predictive models that can improve clinical outcomes.

Research

Perivascular Adipose Tissue Radiomics

Developing automated analysis pipelines for PVAT imaging to predict lung cancer risk. Working with the AADB dataset (31,000+ patients) to identify early biomarkers of malignancy using tissue entropy and texture analysis.

Role: Principal Investigator | Funding: NIH Subaward

Machine Learning in Medical Imaging

Implementing deep learning approaches for automated medical image segmentation and feature extraction. Achieved AUC >0.82 in predicting lung cancer malignancy using radiomic features.
